Infant Vaccines (0-12 Months)

Hepatitis B Vaccine

Infant

Protects against hepatitis B virus that can cause liver disease.

Schedule: Birth, 1-2 months, 6-18 months
Type: Inactivated
Protection: Lifelong
Recommended For:
  • All newborns within 24 hours of birth
  • Infants at 1-2 months
  • Complete series by 6-18 months
  • Special consideration for low birth weight
Efficacy: 95%
Schedule Vaccination

DTaP Vaccine

Infant

Protects against Diphtheria, Tetanus, and Whooping Cough.

Schedule: 2, 4, 6, 15-18 months
Type: Inactivated
Boosters: Preschool and pre-teen
Recommended For:
  • Infants starting at 2 months
  • Five doses by age 6
  • No severe reaction to previous dose
  • Consult for neurological conditions
Efficacy: 80-90%

Toddler & Preschool Vaccines (1-5 Years)

MMR Vaccine

Routine

Protects against Measles, Mumps, and Rubella.

Schedule: 12-15 months & 4-6 years
Type: Live attenuated
Protection: Lifelong
Recommended For:
  • First dose at 12-15 months
  • Second dose at 4-6 years
  • No severe immunodeficiency
  • No gelatin or neomycin allergy
Efficacy: 97% (measles)
Schedule Vaccination

Varicella Vaccine

Routine

Protects against Chickenpox.

Schedule: 12-15 months & 4-6 years
Type: Live attenuated
Protection: Long-term
Recommended For:
  • First dose at 12-15 months
  • Second dose at 4-6 years
  • Children who haven't had chickenpox
  • No severe immune system problems
Efficacy: 98%
Schedule Vaccination

School-age Vaccines (6-12 Years)

HPV Vaccine

School-age

Protects against Human Papillomavirus and related cancers.

Schedule: 11-12 years (2 doses)
Type: Inactivated
Series: 2 doses 6-12 months apart
Recommended For:
  • Boys and girls at 11-12 years
  • Can start as early as 9 years
  • Catch-up to age 26
  • No severe yeast allergy
Efficacy: 99%
Schedule Vaccination

Tdap Booster

School-age

Booster for Tetanus, Diphtheria, and Whooping Cough.

Schedule: 11-12 years
Type: Inactivated
Next Booster: Td every 10 years
Recommended For:
  • All children at 11-12 years
  • Especially before starting middle school
  • Pregnant women with each pregnancy
  • Adults who never received Tdap
Efficacy: 85-90%

Child Vaccine FAQs

Common questions about childhood vaccinations

Yes, childhood vaccines are extensively tested and monitored for safety. They undergo rigorous clinical trials and continuous safety monitoring. The benefits of vaccination far outweigh the risks of vaccine-preventable diseases.

Common side effects include soreness or redness at injection site, mild fever, fussiness, and tiredness. These are normal signs that the immune system is responding and usually resolve within 1-2 days. Serious side effects are very rare.

Yes, getting multiple vaccines during the same visit is safe and recommended. This approach reduces the number of clinic visits and ensures your child is protected against multiple diseases simultaneously. A child's immune system can handle many more antigens than those contained in vaccines.

If your child misses a vaccine dose, we can create a catch-up schedule. It's never too late to get back on track. Contact us to discuss a personalized catch-up plan that will ensure your child receives all necessary protection.
